Frameworks & Methodology

Rigorous frameworks that combine proven impact assessment models with research integrity standards and technology best practices.

CSR Impact Logic Model & Theory of Change

Our CSR impact assessment framework follows a rigorous logic model that tracks the causal chain from inputs to long-term impact. This enables clear attribution and measurement of program effectiveness.

Inputs

Resources invested (funding, staff, infrastructure)

↓

Activities

Program activities and interventions delivered

↓

Outputs

Direct deliverables (people reached, services provided)

↓

Outcomes

Short to medium-term changes (behavior, knowledge, conditions)

↓

Impact

Long-term, sustainable changes in society or environment

KPI Taxonomy: Inputs → Outputs → Outcomes → Impact

Input KPIs

  • Budget allocated
  • Staff hours invested
  • Infrastructure deployed
  • Partnerships established

Output KPIs

  • Number of beneficiaries reached
  • Services delivered
  • Training sessions conducted
  • Materials distributed

Outcome KPIs

  • Knowledge/skill improvements
  • Behavior changes
  • Access improvements
  • Quality of life indicators

Impact KPIs

  • Sustainable social change
  • Environmental improvements
  • Systemic change indicators
  • Long-term beneficiary outcomes

Survey Design Approach

Sampling Strategy

  • Random Sampling: Probability-based selection ensuring representativeness
  • Stratified Sampling: Ensuring representation across key segments
  • Quota Sampling: Targeting specific demographic quotas
  • Sample Size Calculation: Based on confidence level and margin of error

Bias Control

  • Selection Bias: Mitigated through proper sampling methods
  • Response Bias: Addressed through question design and incentives
  • Non-Response Bias: Handled through follow-ups and weighting
  • Social Desirability Bias: Reduced through anonymous surveys and neutral wording

Confidence Intervals

  • Standard confidence level: 95%
  • Margin of error calculation based on sample size
  • Statistical significance testing (p-values)
  • Effect size measurement for practical significance
